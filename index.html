Quaker Worship Groups in New York State Prisons

The New York Yearly Meeting includes 8 worship groups and meetings within New York State Prisons. These meetings bring incarcerated men and women together with attenders from across the state in the spirit of fellowship and community.

Meetings take place once or twice a week, and generally include both silent worship and discussions of social and personal matters. Many groups also undertake projects: writing minutes or articles, planning quarterly meetings, writing and sharing poetry, raps, and essays. We support each other through personal struggles, celebrate accomplishments like the awarding of academic degrees, and help each other build solid foundations for a successful transition from prison to civilian life.

We are looking for new regular attenders at our meetings, but we welcome those who would like to attend even once. You do not have to personally identify as a Quaker to join us.

Information on individual worship groups and how to attend can be found below. For questions on groups not found below, or any more general questions, please email quakersinside@googlegroups.com.

Sing Sing Worship Group

  • Meeting for Worship, Sundays, 8:15am to 11am
  • Meeting with a Concern for Business, Thursdays, 6pm to 8pm

The Sing Sing Worship Group was formed in 19XX. Ten to fifteen attenders gather togther most Thursdays and Sundays for silent worship, discussion, and project planning. Some of the men have been members of the group for years; some will be visiting for the first time at a friend's urging. New visitors from the prison or civilian population are always welcomed in warmly.

Our discussions range over many topics, but frequently concern spiritual growth, what it is to be and become "a man", healing from past struggles and traumas, and bringing our hard-won insights and the best of ourselves back to our communities outside the prison walls.

Sing Sing Correctional Facility is a men's prison in Ossining, New York, 40 miles north of New York City. Metro North's Hudson line serves Ossining Station, which is 1 mile from the prison.

Volunteering in New York State Prisons

Getting approval from the New York State Department of Corrections & Community Supervision to attend our meetings can take a month or more, but it's worth the time and effort. If you have any concerns about the process outlined below, don't hestitate to contact us at quakersinside@googlegroups.com for more information.

Under normal circumstance, the minimum age for a volunteer is 21. Please note that volunteers are prohibited from corresponding, visiting (with the exception of during volunteer activities), or accepting telephone calls from inmates at any DOCCS facility unless they receive permission.

Before first attending a meeting, you will have to apply for "Occassional Volunteer" status with the New York State Department of Corrections & Community Supervision. This will allow you to attend up to six times in a given year. Fill out the Volunteer Registration Form (MFVS3080), parts I and II, and send it to the contact person listed above for the group you'd like to attend.

As part of your application process, the Departmentâ€™s Employee Investigation Unit will perform a criminal background check. You must also be skin tested for TB; this test may be performed by your own physician.

Once you have been approved as an Occassional Volunteer, you may begin attending meetings. Co-ordinate your first visit with the contact person for your group. Occassional Volunteers must arrange for a pass ("gate clearance") with the prison's volunteer co-ordinator before each visit; we recommend submitting your request about a week before you plan to visit.

If you decide to become a regular attender, you can apply for Regular/On-going Volunteer status. This will allow you to attend as many scheduled worship group meetings as you like, and you will not be required to arrange gate clearance before visiting.

Applicants for Regular/On-going Volunteer status will need to attend an orientation session at the prison, and be fingerprinted. Once your application has been approved, you will be issued an official ID card to be kept on file at the prison gate.
